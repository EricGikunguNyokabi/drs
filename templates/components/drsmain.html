DOCUMENT - RETRIEVAL - SYSTEM EXPLAINED

1. Found Lost Document(s) ?

When someone comes across a lost document, they are required to take it to the nearest collection center. Collection centers include police stations, chiefs/sub-chiefs' offices, Huduma centers, or any other office of the presidency across the country.

2. Document Handover at Collection Center

At the collection center, the collection officer receives the document from the individual who found it. The officer enters the details and type of the document(s) and information about the individual who brought it into the system.

3. Compensation Process Initiated

The details of the document and the individual are recorded in the system for the purpose of compensation. Compensation is processed through electronic money transfer to the individual who brought the document.

4. Notification to Document Owner

Upon entering the document details, an SMS notification is automatically sent to the owner of the document. The notification informs the owner about the location of their document and provides an option to request a different collection center if desired.

5. Cost and Collection Information Provided

The SMS also includes information about the fees expected to be charged during the collection process. These fees are typically cheaper than the replacement cost of the document. Additionally, the SMS outlines the collection center details.

6. Publicity and Registration

Publicity efforts are crucial to encourage everyone to download the app or register their details on the portal. This ensures widespread awareness and participation in the Document Retrieval System.
